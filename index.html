Common Threats

Stay vigilant and recognize these common scams targeting bank users

Phishing

Fake emails or websites that trick you into giving your login details.

Smishing

Scam texts pretending to be from banks to steal your info.

Vishing

Scammers call you and pretend to be bank staff to get your OTPs.

Fake Investments

Promises of 'easy profit' or 'double your money' scams.

Account Takeover

Hackers gain access to your account using weak or stolen passwords.

Malicious Apps

Fake apps or unsafe links that steal personal or banking information.

Best Online Banking Practices

Follow these essential security tips to keep your accounts safe

Use Official Apps

Always use official banking apps or websites. Download apps only from official app stores.

Never Share OTP or PIN

Never share your One-time Password (OTP) or PIN with anyone. Banks will never ask for these.

Verify Links

Verify links before clicking. Scammers use fake ones that look similar to real bank websites.

Enable 2FA

Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) whenever possible for an extra layer of security.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi

Avoid logging in using public Wi-Fi networks. Use your mobile data or secure networks instead.

Monitor Regularly

Monitor your account regularly for unusual activity. Report suspicious transactions immediately.

If you encounter a scam, report it immediately to your bank and the National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) Cybercrime Division.
